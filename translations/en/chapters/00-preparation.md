# Preparation

## Summary

A good trip around the Gulf of Saint-Tropez starts with timing. Distances look short, but traffic, parking, beach pressure and reservations decide whether a day feels relaxed. Use this guide as a planner: choose one main area per day, one clear arrival strategy and one alternative for crowds or mistral.

## Best Season

May, June, September and early October are the most comfortable months for a calm guide-based trip. July and August can still work, but only with early starts, booked restaurants and backup beaches.

## Transport

A car remains useful for villages, wineries, trailheads and beaches outside the core. For Saint-Tropez and Port Grimaud, decide day by day whether parking, cycling or a boat connection is less fragile. E-bikes can replace many short car trips, but route advice remains cautious until road surface, crossings and traffic have been checked locally.

## Reservations

Book early for gastronomic restaurants, Pampelonne beach restaurants, popular lunch spots and timed wine experiences. Always include travel time, parking and a slower return in peak traffic.

## Budget

- Basic: parking, simple lunch, water and possibly shuttle or boat.
- Comfort: paid beach setup, reservation, museum or wine tasting.
- Luxury: palace restaurant, valet, beach club or private activity.

Concrete prices should only be used when they come from a current source.

## Packing

Bring water, sun protection, light shoes with grip, swimwear, a power bank and a wind layer. For hikes and bike rides, water, offline maps, a charged phone and realistic heat planning matter more than distance.

## Recommended Base Plan

- Arrival day: accommodation, groceries, short beach or Port Grimaud walk.
- First full day: Prairies de la Mer, Port Grimaud and an easy beach or cycling test.
- Saint-Tropez day: early start or boat plan, with one clear walking route.
- Beach day: Pampelonne or a calmer alternative depending on wind, traffic and parking.
- Village day: Grimaud, Gassin or Ramatuelle with lunch or a viewpoint.

## How to Use This Guide

Start with the type of day, not the longest list of places. Choose one main area and one logistics strategy:

- Calm day: base, nearby beach, Port Grimaud, groceries and an easy dinner.
- Classic Saint-Tropez: early parking or boat, old town, harbour, Citadelle zone and one strong food stop.
- Comfort beach: Pampelonne, La Nartelle, Cavalaire or La Croix-Valmer depending on wind, budget and parking.
- Nature day: Gigaro, Cap Lardier, Escalet, Bonporteau or Domaine du Rayol, with water and heat planning.
- Village day: Grimaud, Gassin or Ramatuelle outside the hottest hours.
- Luxury day: let one reservation shape the whole day.

## One-Week Rhythm From Prairies de la Mer

Use day one for orientation, day two for Port Grimaud and the coast, day three for Saint-Tropez, day four for a major beach, day five for villages, day six for hiking/cycling/Rayol and day seven to repeat the thing that worked best. The guide becomes stronger when you repeat intelligently rather than adding more pins.

## Personal Recommendation

Move less than you think. One well-chosen main place per day often feels more luxurious here than three checked-off stops.
