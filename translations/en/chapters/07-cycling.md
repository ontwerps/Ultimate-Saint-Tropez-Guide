# Cycling

## Summary

This chapter lists the best routes around the Gulf of Saint-Tropez per bike type - road bike, gravel bike and normal bike (trekking/e-bike) - with guide rating, distance, elevation gain, average time and a Komoot link for navigation. All routes start at Camping Prairies de la Mer unless noted otherwise. The key local wisdom: timing decides whether a ride succeeds, not the route. Between 10am and 7pm the coastal roads jam solid in July and August; local riders go out between 6.30 and 9.30am.

The Col de Collebasse (summit 130 m) is the local training climb: 3.1 km from the Ramatuelle side at 3.1% average with a steepest section around 7%, shaded by pines with the sea below. The Col du Canadel (267 m) delivers the finest coastal panorama in the region; its south side looks permanently onto the Mediterranean. The Boucle des Maures over Babaou and Taillude (8 km at 3.25% from Collobrières) is the classic for trained riders: mountain passes, silent back roads and chestnut villages.

The Massif des Maures is one of the best gravel areas in the Var: endless forest tracks through cork oaks and chestnuts. Local gravel riders (gathered around the Maures Gravel club, born from Laurent Biger's routes) treat the ridge track to La Garde-Freinet as the signature ride. Two things only locals check by default: the Plaine des Maures is a nature reserve (stay on authorised tracks), and in summer the prefecture closes entire massifs on high fire-risk days - check the Var department's daily massif-access map before setting off.

The Parcours Cyclable du Littoral (V65) starts practically at the door of Prairies de la Mer, follows an old railway line and is the safest cycling link in the area: 92 of its 120 km are built, 77 km of them on dedicated infrastructure. It is also one of the busiest cycle paths in France (over 310,000 passages per year counted at Grimaud), so expect walkers, skaters and families. East of Sainte-Maxime a 7.2 km section is still missing; there you temporarily ride the D559.

## What locals say

- Timing is everything: from roughly 10am to 7pm the coastal roads jam in July and August, with only a brief lull at lunchtime. Local road cyclists leave before 7am and are home before the heat and the traffic.
- Motorists here are relatively courteous to riders - nearly every French driver has ridden a bike in anger - but the D93 (route des plages) and D559 demand full attention in season.
- Carry two bottles with electrolytes: heat is problem number one. The village fountains of Grimaud, Gassin, Ramatuelle and La Garde-Freinet are the classic refill points.
- Summer rule for the massif: on high fire-risk days the forest tracks are off limits. Locals check the Var massif-access map every evening for the next day.
- Pick your coffee stop like a local: Place des Lices in Saint-Tropez on market day (Tuesday and Saturday), or the terrace in Collobrières halfway around the Boucle des Maures.
- Rental companies in the gulf deliver road, gravel and e-bikes to your accommodation, including Prairies de la Mer; for road bikes, book well ahead in season.

## Personal Recommendation

First ride: the coastal path to Sainte-Maxime, even with children or an e-bike. Best road day: the Collebasse loop early in the morning with a coffee stop in Ramatuelle. Best gravel experience: the ridge line to La Garde-Freinet outside the summer peak. The Boucle des Maures is the queen stage for anyone who can handle 105 km with 1,650 m of climbing.
