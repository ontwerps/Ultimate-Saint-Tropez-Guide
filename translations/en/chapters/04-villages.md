# Villages

## Summary

The villages around the Gulf of Saint-Tropez add calm and height: viewpoints, old lanes, short walks, lunch squares and contrast with beach and harbour.

## Main Villages

- Grimaud village: hill village with castle context and a natural link to Pont des Fées.
- Gassin village: viewpoint village, good for late afternoon and dinner context.
- Ramatuelle village: compact historic stop above Pampelonne, useful with beach or wine.
- Port Grimaud: flat waterside village within Grimaud.
- Saint-Tropez old town: more urban, busier and culturally heavier.

## First Segmentation

- Best views: Gassin, Ramatuelle, Grimaud.
- Best water feeling: Port Grimaud, Saint-Tropez.
- Best beach combination: Ramatuelle, Port Grimaud, Saint-Tropez.
- Best quiet heritage walk: Grimaud with Pont des Fées.
- Best evening context: Gassin or Ramatuelle, depending on restaurant choice.

## Day Planning

For a half-day, choose one village plus coffee, a short walk or a viewpoint. For a full day, combine at most two villages, or one village with beach or wine. In summer, morning and late afternoon are much stronger than hot middays.

## Parking and Accessibility

Parking remains date-sensitive. Check official or local information for car parks, prices, market days and events. Hill villages can mean slopes, steps, cobbles and heat.

## Insider Tip

Plan villages around light. Gassin or Ramatuelle in late afternoon often feels richer than the same place at a hot peak hour.

## Personal Recommendation

Preliminary guide value: high as a calm viewpoint layer between beach days.
