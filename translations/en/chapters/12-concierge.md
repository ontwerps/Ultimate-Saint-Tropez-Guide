# Concierge

## Summary

This chapter turns the guide into practical day plans: arrival day, beach day, market day, cycling morning, hiking day, rainy day and luxury day. Each plan chooses one main area, one logistics strategy and one alternative.

## Day Plans

### Arrival Day

Stay close. Check accommodation, buy groceries, walk to the beach or Port Grimaud and learn how bike, car and practical facilities work.

### Saint-Tropez Day

Start early or arrive deliberately for evening. Choose parking, boat or bike access first, then combine harbour, old town, Citadelle zone and one clear food stop.

### Beach Day

Choose comfort or nature. Pampelonne is iconic but needs timing and budget. La Nartelle, Cavalaire or La Croix-Valmer may be easier. Escalet, Gigaro and Bonporteau require more attention to access, heat and water.

### Village Day

Choose Grimaud for heritage, Gassin for views, Ramatuelle for beach and wine combination, or Port Grimaud for water.

### Cycling Morning

Start with a short coastal test from Prairies de la Mer or Port Grimaud before expanding to Sainte-Maxime, V65 context or the Gassin/Ramatuelle loop.

### Hiking Day

Start early, carry water and avoid heat peaks. Gigaro - Cap Lardier - Cap Taillat is nature-led; Fort-Freinet is heritage-led; Saint-Tropez - Tahiti is lighter coastal context.

### Luxury Day

Let one reservation lead: gastronomic dinner, beach lunch or wine experience. Build the rest of the day around it and keep driving, cycling and return plans sober.

## Verification

Day plans become final only when the underlying restaurants, routes, parking and opening hours are checked.

## Personal Recommendation

The best concierge planning is calm. One main choice per day gives more Saint-Tropez than a map full of pins.
